CASE STUDY #1

Just a Friend-le Wager


TL;DR Up Front

Problem:  Develop a single page web application that fosters IRL, person-to-person interactions.

Process:  Three person collaboration to design, build, test, and launch a Single Page Application to be played concurrently with SuperBowl LIV (a 4-week turnaround time) Included techniques such as Card-sorting, Process flows, wireframing, prototyping, beta-testing, presentation and post-mortem.

Result:  Delivered an engaging product that was used by 50+ people during multiple private SuperBowl watch parties. Currently looking at next steps and development opportunities.

Full Study


PROBLEM
How to create meaningful, in-person relationship interactions between people via technology. The games on the internet and the app store are widely known for escapist games, but how can you use technology to ‘be present’ in the moment and with the people you are with?

PROCESS
Technical Requirements: Technical requirements: use React.js, an SQL Database, and Node.js/express for the backend.

We began with several ideas for how interactions IRL could be made better using basic internet technology, one of which was a way to interact in a bar/restaurant/pub setting, where a mix of known associates, strangers, and close aquaintences would be. We talked about finding ways to integrate a typical chat function and menu website into an evening experience, such as using you phone to request a waiter/drinks, order your food, or communicate with other patrons. We described the similarities between this and the “telephone bar” in which we could all vaguely remember hearing or seeing somewhere but not remembering where (http://www.hilobrow.com/2017/06/04/planet-of-peril-17/) (hint: it’s from seeing cabaret too many times-”The telephone song”)

As we explored the idea, it felt a little too abstract, so we wanted to create a game that would pull people together (friends and strangers alike) to have a shared experience while out at the bar. One team member suggested a “penny bet” game that he had played in which opponent bet pennies on what the next play in a series would be (American football). This sounded promising, as it was exactly the type of low stakes/high engagement activity we were looking for.

The challenge was now how to create a “penny bet” app that is extremely easy to learn, low cost (time and money), that allowed for the most engagement IRL with other patrons who are playing the game.
One major constraint in the process was that our team of three was to be responsible for conceptualizing, designing, building/developing both front and back end, and testing within 4 weeks.
